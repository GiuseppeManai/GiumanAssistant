# Physical AI

Physical AI refers to the integration of artificial intelligence with real-world systems capable of sensing, reasoning, and acting. It combines various technologies to leverage capabilities in diverse applications.

## Overview
Physical AI combines AI, robotics, IoT, and real-world execution to empower machines to perceive, think, and act in complex environments. Key components include:

- **AI / GenAI** (reasoning, planning)
- **Robotics** (execution)
- **IoT and Sensors** (perception)
- **Connectivity** (real-time coordination)

## Key Concepts
- **Digital-to-Physical AI Strategy**: Orchestrating perception, decision, and action in real-world environments.
- **Safety and Governance**: Essential for effective deployment with a principle that emphasizes the need for policy in guiding capability.
- **Embodiment of AI**: Physical AI deals with the embodiment of AI into robots, enhancing real-world interactions and functionalities.

## Applications
Physical AI is utilized in various sectors:
- **Airports**: Autonomous operations, passenger flow management, and security.
- **Healthcare**: Assistive robotics and monitoring systems for elder care.
- **Logistics**: Warehouse automation and last-mile delivery solutions.
- **Security**: Anomaly detection and patrolling.
- **Smart Cities**: Infrastructure and environmental monitoring.

## Challenges
Implementing Physical AI faces several hurdles:
- Ensuring safety and governance.
- Achieving reliable real-time decision-making.
- Coordinating actions between human and machine systems.
- Maintaining reliability in dynamic environments.
